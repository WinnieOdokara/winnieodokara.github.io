Website Evaluations

This page evaluates two websites using the CRAP design principles: Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity.

Apple- Strong Design Example

  • Contrast: Apple does a good job with contrast because the text, images, and buttons are easy to see. The important parts of the page stand out clearly.
  • Repetition: The website repeats the same clean style, colors, fonts, and button designs, which makes the site feel organized.
  • Alignment The text, images, and sections line up well. This makes the page look neat and easy to follow.
  • Proximity: Related information is grouped together, such as product images, short descriptions, and buttons. This makes it easier to understand what each section is about.

Yale School ­of Art-Challenging Design Example

  • Contrast: The website has a lot of different colors, backgrounds, and text styles. Some parts stand out, but other parts feel distracting and hard to read.
  • Repetition: The site repeats some design choices, but the repetition does not always feel consistent. The page feels more experimental than organized.
  • Alignment Some parts of the page do not line up in a clean way. This makes the layout feel crowded and harder to scan.
  • Proximity: Related information is not always grouped together, making it harder to understand the relationships between different elements.

Note: Since this is a website for an art school, some design choices may be intentional or artistic. My evaluation is based only on the CRAP design principles, while recognizing that art and design can be subjective.
